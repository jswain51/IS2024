View of Rome


From 2020-2021, I served an LDS mission in Rome, Italy. It was an amazing experience and I had the time of my life. I got to serve all around the southern half of Italy; in Puglia, Naples, Calabria, Sicily, Rome, and Sardegna! Here, I will share some of my favorite places I went to, and a fun fact about each one. But first about Italy: Italy is an iconic region full of historical literature, art, and architecture. Given the beautiful beaches of Puglia and Sardegna with the mountains of the North in Lombardy and Piedmont, Italy also has picturesque landscapes. And of course the food: pizza, pasta, gelato, pastries and more in all their delectable varieties. All of this, in addition to the fashion, the cars, and all the cultural vibrancy, it is no wonder that Italy is destination unlike any other!

Fun Facts about Each Spot

  1. Rome
    • The Colosseum was commissioned by Emperor Vespasian around 70-72 AD and it was finished in 80 AD, making it over 1900 years old!
    • The Oculus in the roof of the Pantheon is the only source of natural light and ventilation for the building.
    • Vatican City is the smallest country in the world, with a population of 594 and consumes more wine than anywhere else in the world (54.26 litres per resident)!

  2. Campania
    • Naples is home to the very first pizzeria in Italy and even the entire world! It is also where the Margherita pizza was invented in 1889 by Raffaele Esposito.
    • Lemons in Sorrento are considered the best in the world, due to the volcanic soil. Limoncello is a liquor made of alcohol, water, sugar, and the freshest lemons made only in Sorrento.
    • Sentiero degli Dei once served as the sole means of communication between all the villages along the Amalfi Coast!

  3. Sardegna
    • The Sella del Diavolo (or the Devil's Saddle) gets its name from legend. The legend says that Lucifer decided to conquer the land after seeing the beauty of Cagliari's Gulf. But, he was promptly stopped by God, who sent angels to fight Lucifer. During this fight in the skies, Lucifer's horse lost its Saddle after being hit, and the saddle fell into the sea and petrified, creating what we have on earth.
    • Castelsardo was first established in 1270 by the Genoese Doria family. The castle was built during that same period, when the Doria family used as fortification against other fuedal families in the area.
    • Alghero has precious red coral, which has been a economically and culturally significant profession for such a long time that the red coral branch is at the center of the city's coat of arms.
